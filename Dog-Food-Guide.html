Can My Dog Eat This?

A simple guide to what dogs can and cannot eat

Safe for Dogs

  • Carrots: Great for teeth and low in calories.
  • Peanut Butter: A good source of protein (xylitol-free only).
  • Apples: Remove seeds and core, rich in vitamins A and C.
  • Cooked Chicken: Plain, boneless, and unseasoned.
  • Pumpkin: Great for digestion, plain and cooked.
  • Blueberries: Full of antioxidants and fiber.
  • Watermelon: Remove seeds and rind, hydrating treat.
  • Green Beans: Low-calorie, nutritious snack.
  • Rice: Plain cooked rice is easily digestible.
  • Oatmeal: Good fiber source, especially for older dogs.
  • Zucchini: Low calorie and full of nutrients.
  • Plain Yogurt: Provides calcium and probiotics (avoid sugar/artificial sweeteners).
  • Sweet Potatoes: Cooked and plain, great source of fiber and vitamins.
  • Bananas: High in potassium and great for energy.
  • Strawberries: Antioxidant-rich, low-calorie fruit.
  • Spinach: Full of vitamins A, C, and K.
  • Cucumber: Low calorie and hydrating treat.
  • Turkey: Lean, cooked, boneless, and unseasoned.
  • Eggs: Great source of protein and essential nutrients (cooked).
  • Chia Seeds: Full of omega-3 fatty acids and fiber.
  • Watercress: Rich in antioxidants and vitamins.
  • Beets: Great for digestive health and low in calories.
  • Cashews: In moderation, safe for dogs and high in healthy fats.
  • Cauliflower: High in fiber, low in calories.
  • Grapes (in moderation): Beneficial for most dogs, but not for all.
  • Pears: High in fiber, but remove the seeds.
  • Carob: A safe alternative to chocolate.
  • Fennel: Great for digestive issues.
  • Asparagus: Rich in fiber and vitamins.
  • Potatoes: Cooked, plain potatoes are fine, but avoid raw.
  • Almonds: In small amounts, safe for dogs.
  • Cherries: High in antioxidants (without pits).
  • Radishes: Low-calorie treat rich in vitamins.
  • Apricots: In small amounts, they can be safe without the pit.

Unsafe for Dogs

  • Chocolate: Toxic and can be fatal in large amounts.
  • Grapes and Raisins: Can cause kidney failure.
  • Onions and Garlic: Damages red blood cells and can cause anemia.
  • Avocado: Contains persin which can be harmful.
  • Alcohol: Extremely dangerous, even in small amounts.
  • Coffee: Contains caffeine which is toxic to dogs.
  • Macadamia Nuts: Can cause weakness, vomiting, and tremors.
  • Cooked Bones: Can splinter and cause internal injuries.
  • Xylitol: A sweetener found in gum and baked goods, extremely toxic.
  • Yeast Dough: Can expand in the stomach and cause serious issues.
  • Salt: Too much can cause dehydration and sodium ion poisoning.
  • Fat Trimmings: Can cause pancreatitis in dogs.
  • Chives: Related to onions and garlic, harmful to dogs.
  • Rhubarb: Contains oxalates that can be toxic to dogs.
  • Blue Cheese: Contains toxins that can lead to digestive issues.
  • Mustard: Contains compounds that can irritate a dog's digestive system.
  • Tomato Plants: Contain solanine, which is toxic to dogs.
  • Pineapple Cores: Can cause intestinal blockage if swallowed.
  • Spicy Foods: Can cause digestive upset and discomfort.
  • Artificial Sweeteners: Found in gum, baked goods, and candies, toxic to dogs.
  • Red Bell Peppers: Can cause mild irritation in some dogs.
  • Walnuts: Can cause seizures and digestive problems in dogs.
  • Black Walnuts: Toxic to dogs, can cause tremors and seizures.
  • Raw Fish: May contain parasites and toxins harmful to dogs.
  • Hops: Used in brewing beer, can cause fatal reactions in dogs.
  • Cloves: Can cause liver damage and digestive upset.
  • Coconut (flesh): High in fat, can cause digestive issues.
  • Cherries (pit): The pit can cause cyanide poisoning in dogs.
  • Potatoes (raw): Contains solanine, which is toxic to dogs.
  • Persimmons: Can cause gastrointestinal issues.
